# Proposed Site Structure

## Recommended sitemap

1. `index.html`
2. `services.html`
3. `about.html`
4. `contact.html`
5. `thank-you.html`

## UX approach

- Keep the primary navigation short and obvious.
- Put phone and enquiry calls to action high on every page.
- Make it easy for buyers to understand what EIS does within a few seconds.
- Focus on trust, responsiveness, standards, and coverage rather than decorative content.
- Use one clear form with practical fields and no forced marketing opt-in.

## SEO direction

### Home

- Title: `Engineering Inspection Services | Non-Destructive Testing Across the UK`
- H1: `Non-Destructive Testing and Welding Consultancy Across the UK`
- Goal: explain the offer, reassure buyers, and direct them to services or enquiry.

### Services

- Title: `NDT Services | Radiography, UT, MPI, DPI, PMI and Welding Consultancy`
- H1: `NDT Services for Fabrication, Engineering and Inspection Projects`
- Goal: give search engines and users a clearer services page than the current site.

### About

- Title: `About EIS | Inspection Support, Standards and UK Coverage`
- H1: `Practical Inspection Support Built Around Quality and Responsiveness`
- Goal: build trust and explain working standards without overclaiming.

### Contact

- Title: `Contact EIS | Request an NDT Quote or Technical Discussion`
- H1: `Speak to EIS About Your Inspection Requirement`
- Goal: make enquiry conversion simple and direct.

### Thank You

- Title: `Thank You | Engineering Inspection Services`
- H1: `Thank you for your enquiry`
- Goal: confirm submission and route users back to the main site.

## Notes for launch

- Confirm any accreditations before adding them as claims.
- Replace the draft privacy wording with the final privacy policy reference.
- Remove crawler blocking from `robots.txt` and page meta tags before going live.
